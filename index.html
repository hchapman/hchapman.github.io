About Me

I am currently a mathematics graduate student at the University of Georgia working towards a Ph.D. degree. My research interests primarily lie within Topology and Geometry. I am studying under the direction of Jason Cantarella. Recently I have been studying knots from the map-theoretic view of knot diagrams.

During the 2012-2013 school year, I was supported by the University of Georgia's VIGRE II grant. I graduated from Bowdoin College in May of 2011 with a B.A. in Mathematics and Computer Science, with Honors in Mathematics.

Teaching

During the Fall 2015 semester, I'm teaching Calculus I for Science and Engineering (Math 2250).

In the past, I've been instructor for Calculus for Science and Engineering (Spring 2014), and Precalculus (Fall 2013, Fall 2015), and a Writing Intensive Program teaching assistant for a robotics-based Calculus I course (Fall 2015). I've also been a graduate assistant for 1113E, the University System of Georgia's online precalculus course (Fall 2014 — Spring 2015).
